===== Philodendron =====
* originally from rain forest in South America
	* ⇒ warm temperatures, high humidity, indirect light
* belongs to Araceae family
* many different philodendron species, e.g. Philodendron Monstera (Fensterblatt), Philodendron Xanadu or Philodendron Scandens
* many philodendron species are climbing species, benefit greatly from climbing aid as moss stick or coconut stick ⇒ larger and more stable leaves (otherwise also ok)
* watering
	* soil should always be somewhat wet
	* does not need much water
	* sensitive against over-watering
	* water when soil begins to dry out
	* stick finger into soil to test if it's still wet
	* spray species with aerial roots regularly (once per week)
* light
	* no direct sunlight
	* a few meters away from window optimal
	* can even be in hallways with no windows
	* too much light can lead to burnings, too little light can lead to small leaves and long unstable shoots
* soil
	* loose and well aerated
	* mixtures of soil, bark pieces (Rindenstücke), coconut chips, and mineral components such as perlite ensure that roots always receive sufficient oxygen and that moisture is released evenly
	* particularly important to avoid waterlogging, philodendrons are especially sensitive to this
* Propagation
	* easy to propagate from cuttings
	* cut-off piece of shoot with at least one leaf and a node (small knot from which roots emerge)
	* put cutting then directly in water or into loose substrate
	* warm bright location without direct sunlight
	* will root within a few weeks
* problems
	* yellow leaves often due to over-watering
	* dry leaf edges or new leaves not opening properly due to too dry air (often in winter), 50-70% optimal (place in kitchen or in groups)

===== Elephant's foot, Beaucarnea recurvata, ponytail palm =====
* German: Elefantenfuß, Flaschenbaum, Pferdeschwanzpalme, Nolina
* belongs to family of Asparagaceae (aspargus)
* originally from dry, hot region of Mexico
* usually lives very long, easy to handle
* very sensitive to drafts (Zugluft), do not place near windows/doors that are open frequently (stay also away from air conditioners or heaters)
* thick stem stores water
* if it gets too high, complete trunk can be shortened (above thinkened part)
* light
	* loves lot of light
	* can sustain direct sunlight but needs to get used to it when coming from indoors (slowly increase exposure)
* temperature
	* best is 18 - 24 °C
	* can be warmer in growing period
	* should not fall below 10 °C
* humidity
	* loves normal, dry room humidity
	* to high humidity can be problematic
* watering
	* in growth period (march - september), regular but careful watering
	* substrate should be dry before next watering
	* in winter (october - february), water only every 3 or 4 weeks
* problems
	* too long leaves and too limp leaves are due to a too dark location
* soil
	* as a succulent, it needs well-drained substrate, should not compact quickly
	* cactus/succulent soil is well suited 
	* alternatively, potting soil can be mixed with coarse sand or expanded clay (Blähton) in ratio 2:1
	* drainage layer of expanded clay, gravel (Kies), or clay shards (Tonscherben) at the bottom of the pot is essential, as the plant cannot tolerate waterlogging


===== Cambria Orchids =====
* do not grow naturally in the wild
* cross/hybrid between various orchids naturally not crossing, first performed around 1900
* light
	* do direct sunlight
	* ideal is morning or evening sun
* soil
	* cannot tolerate waterlogging
* remove old leaves and blossoms as soon as possible


===== Ficus Benjamina, Weeping Fig (Birkenfeige) =====
* from Asia, natively in Australia
* belongs to //Maulbeerbaumgewächsen// 
* Bonsai possiblity
* like humidity (50% probably fine)
* do not like to move
* if too stressed (overwatering, roots rot, moved a lot), might drop all of its leaves, but they can all come back
* watch out for insects
* light/location
	* very bright, indirect light
	* no drafts (keine Zugluft)
* watering
	* don't like to sit in water
	* do not water too often
* soil
	* fast-draining soil
	* well-aerated
	* cactus soil works nicely
* likes nutrition (seems to result in dark green leaves if well fed)
* verklebte Blätter ⇒ Schildläuse
* once or twice per year showering, put soil/pot in plastic bag to prevent it from getting too wet
