===== Philodendron =====
* originally from rain forest in South America
	* ⇒ warm temperatures, high humidity, indirect light
* belongs to Araceae family
* many different philodendron species, e.g. Philodendron Monstera (Fensterblatt), Philodendron Xanadu or Philodendron Scandens
* many philodendron species are climbing species, benefit greatly from climbing aid as moss stick or coconut stick ⇒ larger and more stable leaves (otherwise also ok)
* watering
	* soil should always be somewhat wet
	* does not need much water
	* sensitive against over-watering
	* water when soil begins to dry out
	* stick finger into soil to test if it's still wet
	* spray species with aerial roots regularly (once per week)
* light
	* no direct sunlight
	* a few meters away from window optimal
	* can even be in hallways with no windows
	* too much light can lead to burnings, too little light can lead to small leaves and long unstable shoots
* soil
	* loose and well aerated
	* mixtures of soil, bark pieces (Rindenstücke), coconut chips, and mineral components such as perlite ensure that roots always receive sufficient oxygen and that moisture is released evenly
	* particularly important to avoid waterlogging, philodendrons are especially sensitive to this
* Propagation
	* easy to propagate from cuttings
	* cut-off piece of shoot with at least one leaf and a node (small knot from which roots emerge)
	* put cutting then directly in water or into loose substrate
	* warm bright location without direct sunlight
	* will root within a few weeks
* problems
	* yellow leaves often due to over-watering
	* dry leaf edges or new leaves not opening properly due to too dry air (often in winter), 50-70% optimal (place in kitchen or in groups)
